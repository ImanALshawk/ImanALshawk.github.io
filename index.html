Police Violence.
Data Visualization.

Every day the police kills 3 people on average

For the past 9 years, the police killed three people on average every day. These figures have been steady throughout the past decade and are not showing any sign of decline. Particularly triking is May 2020, which, amid the protests against police brutality, had the highest number of police shootings in the past 9 years.

The Breakdown of Fatal Police Shootings

We decided to continue exploring our dataset to see the breakdown of all fatal police shootings that took place between 2013 and 2017. From this, we constructed a diagram that splits this data up by gender of the victim, and then further by race and armed status. Interestingly, we quickly noticed that there was not a huge difference between the number of black males that were fatally shot and the number of white males, even though white males make up a much larger proportion of America's population. The difference was even much smaller for the number of black male victims who ended up being unarmed/did not have an actual weapon and the number of white male victims with the same criteria.

Based on our observations from the sankey diagram, we continued to dig further to fully understand the stats behind our initial thoughts. Using data from the U.S. Census Bureau and the World Bank, we compared the percentage of White male victims of fatal police shootings to their overall composition makeup in the United States population and repeated this process for Black male victims. As expected, we found a roughly 1:1 ratio for White male victims, meaning that the proportion of fatal police shooting victims that is White male aligns with the proportion of the United States population that is White male. However, this statistic did not hold true for Black males: whereas Black males only make up 6% of the United States population, they made up around 24% of all fatal police shooting victims, which is 4x greater.

Furthermore, we looked into the close numbers between the number of White male victims who ended up being unarmed/did not have an actual weapon and the number of Black male victims with the same criteria. We found that only 12.5% of all White male victims of fatal police shootings were unarmed, whereas 16.1% of Black male victims ended up being unarmed. Unarmed victims don't usually pose a threat, and yet a larger proportion of Black males were killed while being unarmed. This causes us to believe that there may be some sort of racial bias held by officers when dealing with individuals.

Black teenagers are disproportionately targeted

Young black Americans are disproportionally killed by police. If you set the age range between 15 and 20 years old, you can see that the number of black teenagers killed by police is almost twice that white teenagers. Taking into account that 60% of the American population is white and 15% is black, these figures show that black teenagers face a significantly higher risk of being targeted by the police.
